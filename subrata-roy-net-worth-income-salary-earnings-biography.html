Aamby Valley City and Force India are among the many businesses owned by Subrata Roy Sahara (born 10 June 1948), an Indian businessman who is the founder and chairman of the Sahara India Pariwar, an Indian conglomerate with diversified ownership interests that includes the Grosvenor House in London, the Plaza Hotel in New York, Aamby Valley City, and Force India. Roy established the business in 1978. India Today magazine recognised him as one of the country’s top ten most powerful people in 2012. In 2004, Time magazine referred to the company as “India’s second biggest employer,” after the Indian Railways, and described it as “the second greatest employer in the world.” As part of a criminal investigation into suspected money laundering by the Sahara Group, Sahara was temporarily imprisoned and then freed on bail in 2014 for contempt of court.
